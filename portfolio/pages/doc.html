Documantation:

This site was mostly an experiment to see if I could make parallax.js function as a basic background for a site, and what I could build off of that. I was heavily inspired by Heather Penn's Protean Sea. I don't necessarily intend to share this site, but ideally if I fleshed it out and added my motion graphics portfolio it would be viewed by peers, employers, and potential freelance clients.

My wireframes are in a sketchbook that I left in Connecticut, but as you can see, the basic layout of all the pages is the same. There are three columns (one for each button on the navigation below) and four rows. A header that includes a hamburger menu, content area, button navigation, and the footer. On the homepage all of this is static, but on longer pages the content area can expand and allow for scrolling. All colors and styles were meant to match the hope page and beetle icon. I wanted a consistent, static, small layout that was somewhat reminiscent of a HUD, with the galaxy background. (So it feels like you're looking at the site through a window that leads into space)

Parallax was sourced from Matthew Wagerfield, and the image gallery that takes forever to load and makes the page lag like crazy is called LightGallery and comes from here. The rest of my content is original, but of course I spent a lot of time on W3Schools and StackOverflow when I got stuck.

I spent so much time on the basic requirements that I didn't get to put much energy into going "above and beyond." However, in order to achieve even my most basic vision, I had to learn to implement Parallax.js and use the LightGallery jQuery plugin. Both of these posed problems that compromised the rest of the page/site and took some time to alter, but in the end and despite the loading issue on the gallery page, I'm satisfied with what I achieved.
